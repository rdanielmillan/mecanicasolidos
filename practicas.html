    The physical understanding and formulation of a problem, however, are the most important parts of the solution, and the basic principies still reside in the three steps:

    1. Study of forces and equilibrium requirements.
    2. Study of deformation and conditions of geometric fit.
    3. Application of force-deformation relations.
    Thomas J. Lardner, Preface of the book An Introduction to the Mechanics of Solids: 2nd Ed. with SI Units. Crandall, S. H.; N. C. Dahl; T. J. Lardner. New York, NY: McGraw-Hill, 1999.
